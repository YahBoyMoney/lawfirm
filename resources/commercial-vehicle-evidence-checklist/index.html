Resource guide

Evidence posture

  • Preserve originals
    Keep unedited photos, videos, app screenshots, letters, and records with timestamps where possible.
  • Avoid quick releases
    Do not sign broad releases, authorizations, or settlements without understanding what rights or records may be affected.
  • Request review early
    Evidence can disappear quickly, including dashcam, app, dispatch, maintenance, inspection, and company records.
Crash scene and vehicle proof

Crash scene and vehicle proof

These items may help identify responsible parties, vehicle ownership, route, platform involvement, and coverage issues.

Photos and video

Wide scene photos, vehicle damage, skid marks, road conditions, traffic controls, debris, injuries, and nearby cameras.

Vehicle identity

License plates, DOT numbers, company names, delivery logos, fleet markings, trailer numbers, or rideshare trip details.

Witness and report details

Names, phone numbers, report numbers, exchange forms, incident numbers, and officer or agency information.

App and trip records

Pickup/drop-off data, route screenshots, app messages, receipts, order details, and driver or company information.

Company, insurance, and medical organization

Company, insurance, and medical organization

A reviewer may need to understand the parties, injuries, records, and communications without receiving a sensitive dump through a public form.

Insurance communications

Claim numbers, adjuster names, letters, requests for recorded statements, medical authorization forms, and settlement offers.

Medical timeline

ER, urgent care, hospitalization, imaging, surgery, therapy, specialists, missed work, and current symptoms summarized by date.

Preservation concerns

Dashcam, telematics, phone records, dispatch logs, maintenance records, driver qualification, inspection, and delivery/platform records.

Deadline-sensitive facts

Government vehicles, public entities, minors, out-of-state parties, employers, commercial carriers, or already scheduled hearings.
