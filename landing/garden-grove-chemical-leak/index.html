Proof checklist

Preserve the records that make a screening useful.

Documented evacuations, expenses, symptoms, and losses move a screening forward. Save the records below before you send a short summary. Do not send privileged or highly sensitive documents through the public form.

01

Evacuation and location

  • Evacuation alert screenshots with time stamps
  • Home, work, school, or business address or cross streets
  • Hotel, shelter, gas, childcare, and pet boarding receipts
  • Mileage notes for displaced commuting
02

Symptoms and health

  • Symptom timeline with dates, locations, and exposure events
  • Urgent care, ER, hospital, or primary-care visit summaries
  • Prescriptions, follow-up instructions, and referrals
  • Pediatric records for affected children
03

Work and business loss

  • Missed work records, payroll stubs, and PTO use
  • Closure notices, customer messages, and rescheduled bookings
  • POS reports, refunds, voided orders, and chargebacks
  • Spoiled inventory, perishable goods, and supplier notes
04

Property and cleanup

  • Time-stamped photos and short videos of conditions
  • Cleaning, HVAC, and remediation invoices
  • Odor logs, residue notes, and air-quality readings
  • Damaged furnishings, signage, equipment, or vehicles

FAQ

Common questions from affected residents and businesses.

General information only, not legal advice. Facts, deadlines, representation status, proof, and damages determine whether a matter can be reviewed, referred, accepted, or declined.

Call 909-609-6685 if your matter is urgent or deadline-sensitive.

Can I request screening if I only evacuated?

Yes. Evacuation-only claims may be lower value than documented medical or business-loss claims, but receipts, shelter records, hotel costs, gas, childcare, pet boarding, missed work, address proof, and alert screenshots can still matter.

What if I had symptoms?

Document when symptoms started, where you were, what you smelled or saw, who else was affected, and whether you sought urgent care, ER care, prescriptions, or follow-up. Pediatric, asthma, and respiratory histories are worth noting.

Can a business or school program submit?

Yes. Business proof may include closure notices, payroll disruption, POS reports, canceled appointments, refund logs, inventory loss, supplier disruption, and cleaning costs. Schools and after-school programs can use the same checklist.

What if I already signed with another lawyer?

Say so on the form. Berhe Jones will not contact a person already represented by counsel about the same matter unless the rules of professional conduct allow it.

Does the screening cost anything?

The initial screening is free. Representation, fees, costs, and scope are governed only by a signed written engagement agreement after conflicts review.

How fast should I act?

Act now to preserve proof. Deadlines vary by facts, defendants, government involvement, insurance, and claim type. Some claims against public entities have early notice requirements. Call if urgent.
