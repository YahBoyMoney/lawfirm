Proof checklist

Save the records that make a screening useful.

The strongest leads are not just names and numbers. They include address proof, documented exposure, real losses, medical or business records, and clear timelines.

Evacuation and location

  • Evacuation alert screenshots
  • Address or cross streets
  • Hotel, shelter, gas, childcare, or pet receipts

Symptoms and health

  • Symptom timeline
  • Urgent care, ER, or doctor summaries
  • Prescriptions and follow-up instructions

Work and business loss

  • Missed work records
  • Closure notices and payroll notes
  • POS reports, refunds, canceled appointments

Property and cleanup

  • Photos or videos
  • Cleaning invoices
  • Odor, residue, air-quality, or inventory notes

FAQ

Common screening questions from affected residents and businesses.

General information only, not legal advice. Facts, deadlines, representation status, proof, and damages determine whether a matter can be reviewed, referred, accepted, or declined.

Can I request screening if I only evacuated?

Yes. Evacuation-only claims may be lower value, but receipts, address proof, shelter records, gas, childcare, pet boarding, missed work, and alert screenshots can still matter.

What if I had symptoms?

Document when symptoms started, where you were, what you smelled or saw, who else was affected, and whether you sought urgent care, ER care, prescriptions, or follow-up.

Can a business submit?

Yes. Business proof may include closure notices, payroll disruption, POS reports, cancelled appointments, refund logs, inventory issues, and cleaning costs.

What if I already signed with another lawyer?

Say so in the form. Berhe Jones will not contact someone represented by counsel about the same matter unless the rules allow it.

Does screening cost money?

The initial screening is free. Representation, fees, costs, and scope require conflicts review and a signed written agreement.

How fast should I act?

Act now to preserve proof. Deadlines vary by facts, defendants, government involvement, insurance, and claim type. Call if urgent.
