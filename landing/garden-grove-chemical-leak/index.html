Garden Grove chemical leak FAQ

Questions affected residents, renters, workers, parents, and business owners are asking

These answers are general information and not legal advice. Facts, deadlines, representation status, insurance, damages, and proof determine whether a claim can be reviewed, referred, accepted, or declined.

Can I request screening if I evacuated but did not go to a doctor?

Yes. Evacuation-only claims may be lower value than documented injury or business-loss claims, but receipts, shelter records, hotel costs, gas, childcare, pet boarding, missed work, and address proof can still matter.

What if I had symptoms after the methyl methacrylate incident?

Document when symptoms started, what you smelled or saw, where you were, who else was affected, and whether you sought urgent care, ER care, prescriptions, or follow-up. Do not upload full medical records through the public form.

Can a small business submit a screening request?

Yes. Business interruption proof can include closure notices, payroll disruption, POS reports, cancelled appointments, refund logs, staff messages, inventory issues, cleaning costs, and customer communications.

What if I already signed with another lawyer?

Say so in the form. Berhe Jones will not contact someone represented by counsel about the same matter unless the rules allow it. The first step is conflict and representation-status screening.

How much does the screening cost?

The initial screening is free. If Berhe Jones accepts a matter, representation, fees, costs, and scope must be governed by a signed written agreement. No recovery or result is promised.

How fast should I act?

Act now to preserve proof. Legal deadlines vary by facts, defendants, government involvement, insurance issues, and claim type. If there is an urgent deadline or safety issue, call 909-609-6685 instead of waiting to send a long form message.
