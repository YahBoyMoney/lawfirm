Who should document now

Useful screening signals

  • Evacuation or displacement
    Evacuation alerts, shelter/hotel stays, temporary relocation, transportation, food, childcare, pet boarding, or cleanup costs.
  • Health symptoms or medical care
    Coughing, throat or eye irritation, headaches, dizziness, wheezing, chest tightness, rash, breathing issues, urgent care, ER visits, or vulnerable household impacts.
  • Work or business interruption
    Missed shifts, lost wages, closed storefronts, cancelled appointments, payroll disruption, lost inventory, or documented revenue loss.
What to save

Keep proof before memories fade or receipts disappear

Stronger reviews usually have documents. Keep originals, screenshots, photos, and dates. Do not send sensitive full records through public forms.

Evacuation proof

Emergency alerts, text notices, maps, hotel confirmations, shelter records, gas, food, childcare, pet boarding, and transportation receipts.

Medical proof

Visit dates, discharge papers, prescriptions, symptom notes, photos, and names of treating providers. Keep full records private until a secure route is provided.

Wage and business loss

Schedules, payroll records, missed shifts, POS reports, appointment cancellations, refund records, inventory loss, and customer messages.

Property and cleanup

Photos of odors, residue, filters, HVAC issues, cleanup supplies, damage, and any professional testing or remediation invoices.

Location connection

Affected home, work, school, business, or caregiving address, plus dates and times connected to evacuation or exposure.

Prior lawyer status

If you already signed with a lawyer, speak with that lawyer. This screening is not meant to interfere with existing representation.
